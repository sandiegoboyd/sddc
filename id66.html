The search for a new family addition takes time. When you are ready both mentally and enviromentally, your quest begins. Whether after the loss of a loved one or the introduction of your first. The enviroment for the pet is the most important aspect to have ready before any intoduction.
 
If you are a animal person you know this but we need to review anyway. Adequate and proper food is a must. Using guidlines from whom you get the animal can teach a lot about how well the dog has been treated and potential heath responsiibilites you are undertaking.
 
Secure housing comes next. Access to things your dog could get into trouble with must be addressed. No access to streets or alleys, a secure yard/area gives your pet safety and you reassurance your pet is well taken care of.
 
A good dachshund Veterinarian is a strict requirement. SDDC can steer you to one's we use, sometimes even dachshund oriented.
 
Dog training before/during/after pet ownership is a reasonable cost, and gives you a lifetime of ability to control your pet now and in the future with new pets. Training is not pet specific, it applies to all dogs you encounter. SDDC can make recommendations here.
 
Visit the Member Breeders page here; http://sddc.us/id4.html, to see if there is a breeder in your area. Contact them about pedigree as well as rescue pets.
 
See the Dachshund Rescue page; http://sddc.us/id27.html, for our 2 rescue groups contact info. Follow their instuctions to be put on the adoption list.
 
You can also contact your local Humane Society and SPCA. Many fine dogs, sometimes dachshunds are in desperate need of loving care. The San Diego Humane Society and SPCA calendar of events is available here. http://sddc.us/id43.html.
 
Hope this helps in your search. If you have additional questions you may use the form below and good luck. Having a dachshund in your life is the most rewarding endeavor you can undertake.

Mike Martin, Webmaster
