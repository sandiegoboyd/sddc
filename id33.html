Vernal EquiDox Walk in the Park & "Bark-in-the-Park-2-Concert-for-the-Animals"

Dachshunds gathering for the Vernal EquiDox Walk
Vernal EquiDox 2008
Photos by Ralph Petrozello
Spreckels Organ Pavilion Concert Audience
The Pavilion holds 4000 people
Sunday, April 6th was the date set for our "Vernal EquiDox Walk in the Park", which we coordinated this year with
Carol Williams "Bark-in-the-Park-2" Concert for the Animals. This was her fund raising concert for the San Diego Humane Society. She also would be inviting all of the dogs and their owners to parade across the 'red carpet'
on the stage as he played animal themed music, and allow them to get their "15 seconds of fame".

KUSI was broadcasting live from Balboa Park from 7am to 10am, with Dave Scott the weatherman, for the 'Good Morning San Diego' program. He was there to help promote Carol Williams 'Concert for the Animals', "Bark-in-the-Park-2".

Carol has gone out of her way on a number of occasions
and has accommodated the San Diego Dachshund Club by announcing our events at her concerts, and allowing us to
parade our Doxies across the stage as she played a musical request especially for us.

This was a great opportunity to help support Carol in her efforts, and all she has done for the club, and this was also a golden opportunity for the Dachshund Club to get some 'live on-air publicity' as well.
Ralph actually "borrowed" two Doxies (Loaner Wieners?) for the weekend to bring down to the live broadcast.
They got down to the park at 7am. KUSI cut to Dave Scott about every 15 minutes or so from 7:00 to 10:00.
Dave asked Ralph the names of the dogs that he had brought, and when he said "Ricky and Lucy"
Dave said ....."Lucy, you got some splainin' to do".

Doxies, along with the other breed dogs that showed up, were also in a number of the other shots as Dave interviewed Carol, or while she played, and he promoted the event, which was scheduled to start at 2:00 on Sunday.
The newscaster interviewed the people and showed the
Doxies numerous times on camera.

We had a tremendous turnout at the 'Vernal Equidox' and there were thousands of people at the concert.
The parade was for all breeds of dogs, but the "Dachshunds Dominated the Day" and the parade,
since we had gathered an hour earlier up by the main fountain for the 'Vernal EquiDox Walk' down the Prado.
The tourists were tremendously amused and baffled at the
site of all the Dachshunds.

Board President Sue Rucker, and Pat Wheeler, Treasurer, were at the EquiDox Walk and the Organ Concert
with their dogs too. The club showed our support and appreciation. They had a donation for Carols event from the Dachshund Club, and we were all asked
to come up on the stage to make the presentation. We thanked Carol Williams for all she has done for The San Diego Dachshund Club.

All in all it was a very beautiful day in the park, and there were many happy faces on the dogs and people as well.

Carol Williams photographer, Robert Harrington, has posted some of the photos from the organ concert and dog parade on her web site.

If everyone with a dog had got up and walked across the stage, there would have been hardly anyone left in the audience!
