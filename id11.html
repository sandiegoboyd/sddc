Dog Laws in San Diego

This is meant to be a guide from the San Diego Dachshund Club to use to find dog laws for San Diego and the state of California. It is not intended to be used as a source of legal advice. If you need further information about laws, contact the San Diego County Public Law Library at www.sdcpll.org.

Introduction - State laws apply to everyone throughout the state. San Diego County Codes govern if you live outside of San Diego city limits and do not live in another incorporated area (such as El Cajon, Chula Vista, Escondido, etc). If you live in an incorporated area, then regulations in that area's municipal code govern.

I. State Laws - Here is where to find basic laws about dogs for the State of California:
Bites- Civil Code 3342, Penal Code 597b
Dog Licenses- Food & Agriculture 30801
Dangerous Dogs- Food & Agriculture 31621
Breeders- Health & Safety 122300
Pet Dealers- Health & Safety 122125
You can find California state laws online by going to http://www.leginfo.ca.gov/calaw.html. When you get there, click on the code you want to search. Then scroll down and put in the number or keyword and hit "Search". This is an example of your tax dollars at work because this site is maintained by the California State Legislature and is updated continually.

II. San Diego County Laws - These laws apply if you live outside of San Diego city limits and do not live in another incorporated area (such as El Cajon, Chula Vista, Escondido, etc). The San Diego County Code of Regulatory Ordinances is available online at http://www.amlegal.com/sandiego_county_ca/. San Diego's Animal Control site is available at: http://www.co.san-diego.ca.us/cnty/cntydepts/community/animal_control/index.html and they have an excellent Frequently Asked Question list.

Biting- Must keep dog from biting and if dog does bite, owner can be charged with a misdemeanor- San Diego County Code section 62.669.1. Owner of unaltered dog that bites someone has to pay a fine and an "altering deposit". If the dog is altered within 30 days, the owner gets the $$ back. If the dog is not altered in this time, the deposit is forfeited to Animal Control Services. (San Diego County Code section 62.679). Popular site- http://www.dogbitelaw.com/

Disturbing the Peace - Defined as frequent or long continued noise that disturbs a person of normal sensitivity.
Those refusing to control their dogs/pets can be charged with a misdemeanor. San Diego County Code 62.672.
If you believe the dog is barking because it is suffering or in distress, contact the San Diego County Animal Control department's emergency number (619) 236-2341 or in the North County (760) 438-1460 (these are 24 hour emergency numbers). All other animal noise complaints may be reported during normal business hours as follows:
Unincorporated areas: 858-694-3741
Carlsbad: 760-438-2312
Del Mar: 858-694-3741
Encinitas: 858-694-3741
Escondido: 760-746-7307
Poway: 858-748-6600
San Diego: 619-236-5500
San Marcos: 760-438-2312
Santee: 858-565-5200
Solana Beach: 858-694-3741


Complaints - identity of anyone who complains of a violation of these laws will be kept confidential to the extent permitted by law. San Diego County Code 62.675

Dogs in Cars - Can have dog loose in vehicle but if they windows are open, must have animal crated or tethered so that it can't get out of the motor vehicle- San Diego County Code 62.700. Can't leave dog in car without adequate ventilation or in conditions which adversely affect the animal's health or welfare. San Diego County Code 62.701

Female Dogs in Season - Must be kept in an enclosure to prevent attracting male dogs. - San Diego County Code section 62.671

Inhumane Treatment - Inhumane treatment and abandonment- no person shall treat an animal in a cruel or inhumane manner or torture or cause pain. San Diego County Code 62.673

Leash Laws - Dogs must be kept on leashes while in public- San Diego County Code section 62.669 (a). Dogs must be restrained on private property by a fence, tether, lease, etc in a humane manner- San Diego County Code section 62.669 (b).

Nuisance - Dogs are not allowed to defecate or urinate on the property of another. It is the owner's job to curb the dog and immediately remove any feces to a proper receptacle. San Diego County Code 62.670

III. City of San Diego Laws - These laws apply if you live within San Diego city limits. If you live in a different city, you must obey those laws. The San Diego Municipal Code (SDMC) can be found online at http://clerkdoc.sannet.gov/Website/mc/mc.html
Dogs on Beaches - Dogs can't be on public beaches between 9 a.m. and 6 p.m. from April 1- Oct 31 and 9 a.m. to 4 p.m. Nov 1 - March 31. SDMC 63.20.12. However, dogs can be unleashed on Fiesta Island in Mission Bay Park
Fees and Permits- Dog license fees- SDMC 44.0322
Leash Laws- Dogs must be on leashes- Dogs in city parks and beaches must be kept on leashes per SDMC section 63.0102(b)(2), but leash can be up to 8 feet long. SDMC section 63.0102(b)(2)(B). Dogs can be off their leashes if they are in dog park. SDMC section 63.0102(b)(2).
Nuisance - Dogs are not allowed to defecate or urinate on the property of another. It is the owner's job to curb the dog and immediately remove any feces to a proper receptacle. SDMC 44.0304.1

Other Info - Limits on Number of Dogs- As of March 12, 2004 in Oakland, CA, city officials are considering an ordinance to limit the number of dogs in most households to four. (source- Union Tribune 3/12/04). City and County of San Diego, limit is 6 adult dogs. Santee - 2 adult dogs, La Mesa - 2 adult dogs.

Custody of Pets after Divorce- http://www.petcustody.com/, http://www.divorcesource.com/webcartd/dl99jun113.html

Pet Wills / Trusts-
An Introduction to Pet in Wills and Pet Euthanasia by Suzette Daniels, Michigan State University - Detroit College of Law, 2004. Available online at www.animallaw.info/articles/arusdanielssuzette2004.htm

Separation, Custody, and Estate Planning Issues Relating to Companion Animals by Professor Rebecca J. Huss, 74 Univ. Colo. Law Review 181, 2003. Available online at: www.animallaw.info/articles/arus74colorev181.htm

Conclusion - If you need help in finding laws for other cities, states, or counties, contact the San Diego County Public Law Library at www.sdcpll.org or call (619) 531-3900.
